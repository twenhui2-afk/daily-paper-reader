Introduction 

Topological quantum field theories (TQFTs) were axiomatized by Atiyah [Ati88] as symmetric monoidal functors from the category of ( n + 1)–dimensional cobor-disms to the category of vector spaces. In dimension three, a fundamental ex-ample is the Turaev–Viro TQFT, which is constructed from semisimple spherical tensor categories [TV92, BW99]. This theory provides a state-sum model for 3-manifold invariants and has been extensively studied in various contexts. Over the past two decades, substantial progress has been made in extending the Tu-raev–Viro type theories to non-semisimple spherical type categories, see for exam-ple: [AG20, CGPT20, GP13, GPT11]. A related line of research concerns skein theoretic methods in quantum topology. Skein modules provide algebraic models for spaces generated by embedded graphs in a manifold, subject to local relations. One of the most influential examples is the Kauffman skein algebra of a surface, introduced independently by Przytycki [Prz91, Prz99] and Turaev [Tur91]. Its definition uses relations derived from the Jones polynomial, or equivalently the Kauffman bracket. Although this construction is combinatorial in nature, it encodes deep geometric and algebraic information and has been extensively studied; see for instance [BW11, BFK99, DKS25, FKL19, GJS23]. More recently, skein-theoretic techniques have been adapted to the non-semisimple framework and lead to the construction of TQFTs, see [GKP22, CGHP23, CGP23, CGPV23, BH26, Hai25, MSWY26]. Admissible skein modules have also been re-interpreted as the images of so-called ansular functors in [MW24b]. One of the main tools of this theory is modified traces on ideals in pivotal categories. In [CGPV23] admissible skein modules associated with such ideals were introduced and used to construct “non-compact” (2 + 1)-dimensional TQFTs (i.e. TQFTs defined on the category of cobordisms such that, for each connected component, the incoming boundary is nonempty). The purpose of this article is to generalize those results to a G-graded setting where G is a group, not necessarily abelian. Before discussing this in more detail, let us recall that another natural extension of TQFTs arises from homotopical refinements. Turaev introduced the notion of a homotopy quantum field theory (HQFT), in which manifolds and cobordisms are equipped with maps to a fixed target space, see [Tur00, Tur10, TV12, TV14, TV17, SV23]. When the target is an Eilenberg–MacLane space K(G, 1), this structure amounts to decorating manifolds with (non-commutative) G-valued cohomology classes, leading to the notion of a G-HQFT. Such theories are closely related to G-graded tensor categories and provide a natural framework for quantum invariants with homotopical data.

to use it to produce (2 + 1)-dimensional G-HQFTs (see Definition 2.4). Our results can be organized into three main themes. First, we formulate a G-graded analogue of admissible skein modules. This is a generalization of the main result of [DST24] to non-semisimple and non-finite cases, using rather different techniques. For a G-graded pivotal category equipped with an ideal, we define skein modules generated by ribbon graphs whose colorings respect the grading data. The resulting graded skein modules carry natural actions of mapping class groups compatible with the grading. It should be noted that these graded admissible skein modules first appeared in [CGP23] and are generalizations of the admissible skein modules introduced in [CGPV23]. However, referees sug-gested that admissible skein modules should be presented with their corresponding topological applications, so we reorganized our presentation of these results and we will not publish [CGP23] but include its results in this paper and [CGPV23] with their corresponding topological applications. In particular, we include the G-graded results here in full detail since that work will not be published elsewhere.
