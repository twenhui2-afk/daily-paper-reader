DECIDABILITY OF SINGULARITIES IN THE EKEDAHL–OORT STRATIFICATION     

> JEAN-STEFAN KOSKIVIRTA, LORENZO LA PORTA
> Abstract. For an abelian type Shimura variety and an odd prime pof good reduction, we characterize the regularity in codimension one of Zariski closures of Ekedahl–Oort strata in terms of the Frobenius action on the root datum. We give an algorithm that detects codimension one singularities for arbitrary Ekedahl–Oort strata. When the Shimura datum is of split type, we relate the singularities of Ekedahl–Oort strata to a stack of G-zips over the complex numbers. We study the existence of generalized Hasse invariants on this stack.

The Ekedahl–Oort (EO) stratification on the special fiber of a Shimura variety was first introduced by Ekedahl and Oort [Oor01], in the case of Siegel modular varieties. This was subsequently generalized by the work of many [VW13, Zha18, SZ22, IKY24]. It has proved a very effective tool to study the geometry of such moduli spaces. For example, the theory of generalized Hasse invariants on EO strata in [GK19a, Box15] made it possible to extend the construction of Galois representations attached to more general automorphic representations. The singularities appearing in the EO strata encode interesting arithmetic information. For this reason, it is an important problem to detect and measure such singularities. In this paper, building on the results of [KLPR25], we address these questions. Let us explain in further detail. Fix p an odd prime. Write S for the Fp-fiber at some p-hyperspecial level K of an abelian type Shimura variety. Denote the EO strata in S by (Sw)w∈I W , where I W is a certain subset of the Weyl group of the relevant reductive group G, §2.1.8. Here, I denotes the type of the Hodge parabolic P ⊆ G attached to the Shimura datum. The smooth and normal loci of the Zariski closure Sw can be written as unions of EO strata. It is a difficult problem to determine which EO strata appear therein. In the recent preprint [KLPR25], the authors together with S. Reppen gave a criterion for the normality of a locally closed subcheme U ⊆ S which is a union of EO strata. Such a subcheme is called an elementary w-open if 

U = Sw ∪ Sw′ , where Sw′ has codimension one in Sw. This condition is equivalent to w′ ∈ ΓI (w), the set of lower neighbors of w in I W with respect to a certain partial order ≼I , see §2.1.10. For elementary w-open subschemes U , the authors gave a list of explicit conditions that are equivalent to the smoothness of U (itself equivalent to normality, in this setting).
