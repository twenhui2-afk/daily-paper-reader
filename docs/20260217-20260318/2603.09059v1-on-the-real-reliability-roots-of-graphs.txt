# On the Real Reliability Roots of Graphs 

## Jason I. Brown and Isaac McMullin Department of Mathematics and Statistics, Dalhousie University, Halifax, Canada March 11, 2026 

Abstract 

Consider a connected graph G, and assume that every edge fails independently with probability q. The (all-terminal) reliability polynomial is the probability in q

that the spanning connected subgraph of operational edges is connected. In this pa-per we focus on the real roots of reliability polynomials ( reliability roots ). We prove that almost every graph has a nonreal reliability root, and that the reliability poly-nomials of graphs have roots dense on the interval rβ, 0s where β « ´ 0.5707202942. 

Keywords : graph, reliability, roots, real-rootedness, corank, split reliability

# 1 Introduction 

A multigraph G “ p V, E q consists of a finite vertex set V and a finite edge multiset E

consisting of unordered pairs of elements of V (the order and size of G are, respectively, 

|V | and |E|). A simple graph, or simply a graph , is one where E is a set (that is, there are no multiple edges). For standard graph theory terminology we refer the reader to [16]. There are many ways to model a network’s robustness against random failure. One of the most common ways is as follows. Suppose that every vertex of a multigraph G (we assume from here on in that all multigraphs and graphs are always connected) is always “operational” but each edge independently fails with probability q P r 0, 1s (so each edge is independently operational with probability p “ 1 ´ q). Indeed the coefficients of various expansions of the reliability polynomial had all been conjec-tured to be unimodal (see [10]), and only recently has this been confirmed for some sequences [1, 14], with proofs using deep techniques from algebraic geometry. A famous – and beautiful! – theorem due to Newton (see, for example [11]) proves that if a polynomial with positive coefficients has all real roots (i.e. is real-rooted ) then the coefficient sequence is unimodal. This observation has led many to explore the real (and complex) roots of various graph polynomials, including chromatic polynomials , matching polynomials , independence polynomials , domination polynomials , and indeed reliability polynomials (see, for example, [2, 4, 8, 9, 12, 13]). And indeed, while some graph polyno-mials, such as matching polynomials, always have all real roots, most graph polynomials have real and nonreal roots, and characterizing when the roots are always real (i.e. the polynomial is real-rooted) has been of considerable interest. Some families of graphs, such as trees and cycles (the graphs of respectively corank 

d “ m ´ n ` 1 of 0 and 1) have all real reliability roots. For corank d “ 2, there are graphs that have nonreal reliability roots. The Θ-graph, consisting of two vertices x and

While it is not the case that every reliability root is real, in [4] it was shown that for any multigraph G there exists a subdivision G1 (and hence infinitely many subdivisions) such that the reliability roots of G1 are all real. Despite this, the question of how common real-rootedness is for reliability polynomials has never been explored. 2The closure of the real roots of reliability polynomials of multigraphs is precisely 

r´ 1, 0s Y t 1u [4]. However, the proof in [4] uses multigraphs in an essential way, and so the corresponding problem for graphs seems much more subtle and difficult. This paper will address two main questions: 1. How common is real-rootedness for reliability polynomials of graphs? 2. What is the closure of the real roots of reliability polynomials of graphs?

# 3 Density of the Real Reliabity Roots of Graphs 

It is known [4] that all real reliability roots of multigraphs are bounded, and are indeed in r´ 1, 0q Y t 1u. As well, in fact for multigraphs , the region is covered – the closure of real reliability roots for multigraphs is in fact all of r´ 1, 0s Y t 1u (we point out that to get the closure result, multiple edges play an essential role in the construction). This contrasts with the complex roots, which are only conjectured to be bounded. In terms of the closure of the real reliability roots of graphs, the question seems more subtle than for multigraphs. What is not clear is whether the set of reliability roots of multigraphs strictly contains the set of reliability roots of graphs. Perhaps surprisingly, in [5] it was shown that indeed the two sets differ, in that q “ ´ 1, which is the reliability root of many multigraphs, is never the reliability root of a graph (it is not hard to see that given any multigraph G of order at least 2, replacing each edge by a bundle of 2 k edges transforms the reliability polynomial by replacing q by q2k, and hence q “ ´ 1 becomes a root as 1 is always a root of a reliability polynomial). It seems natural then to focus on graphs. Computations have led us to conjecture that the closure of the real reliability roots of graphs is in fact r´ 1, 0s Y t 1u as well, but a proof has been elusive. Is it perhaps the case in general that almost every graph has exactly 1 or 0 real roots? In Section 3 we provided an interval of width more than 1 {2 contained in the closure of the real reliability roots of graphs, but we conjecture that indeed the region should be all of r´ 1, 0s Y t 1u. We already know that ´1 is not a reliability root of a graph [5], but perhaps ´1 is in the closure of the roots of reliability polynomials of graphs. Calculations show that the reliability polynomials of complete graphs have real roots approaching ´1, but a closed form of Rel pKn; qq is not known, the observation remains tantalizingly out of reach at the moment.
