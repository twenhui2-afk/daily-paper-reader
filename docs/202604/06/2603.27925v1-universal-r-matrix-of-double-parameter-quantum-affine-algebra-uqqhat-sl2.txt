1. I ntroduction 

Solutions of the Yang-Baxter equations have played an essential role in physics and mathematics, such as statistical mechanics, knot theory, conformal field theory, etc (see [21], [19], [13] etc). A solution of a Yang-Baxter equation is called an R-matrix .Since the introduction of quantum groups by Drinfeld [7] and Jimbo [11] in the 1980s, the universal R-matrices [7] have continued to play a central role in numerous areas of mathematics and physics. For the finite dimensional representation σ of a quantum group 

Uq(g), we know the existence of R-matrix associated with σ as an image of the universal 

R-matrix R. However, in many cases, it is not easy to obtain its concrete form via a direct use of R. The well-known alternative method is the Jimbo’s intertwiner method [11]. Variations of quantum groups, such as multi-parameter quantum groups [12, 17, 18], quantum superalgebras [10, 15, 16, 23, 24], and the Drinfeld doubles of Nichols algebras of diagonal type [1, 2, 3, 9], have also been studied.
