OPTIMIZING RIESZ MEANS OF ROBIN LAPLACE OPERATORS ON CUBOIDS IN A SEMICLASSICAL LIMIT 

MATTHIAS BAUR AND SIMON LARSON 

Abstract. We study asymptotic shape optimization for Riesz means of Robin Laplacian eigenvalues among cuboids of fixed measure. Our focus is the regime where the Robin parameter is proportional to the square root of the spectral parameter defining the Riesz means. Here, a transition emerges based on the precise ratio between the two parameters: as the spectral parameter tends to infinity, sequences of maximizers shift from converging to the unit cube to lacking convergent subsequences entirely. Key tools include two-term spectral asymptotics and uniform inequalities for the Riesz means. Notably, the transition point governing the behavior of optimizers may differ from the point at which the second asymptotic term changes sign. This shows that heuristics based solely on asymptotics for a fixed domain fail to accurately predict the asymptotic behavior of maximizers.

For Riesz means of the Dirichlet Laplacian −∆DΩ , bounds of this form were obtained independently by Berezin [5] and Li–Yau [30] for any open Ω ⊂ Rd of finite measure as soon as γ ≥ 1. Also for γ ≥ 1, Kr¨ oger [25] proved that the reverse inequality holds for Riesz means of the Neumann Laplacian −∆NΩ. A well-known, and unresolved, conjecture of P´ olya [31] posits that these inequalities remain valid for γ = 0 and any Ω. For domains which tile Rd, and thus in particular for cuboids, P´ olya himself proved the conjecture [32]. As far as we are aware, Theorem 1.3 is the first result concerning such uniform inequalities when the Robin parameter is coupled to the spectral parameter. By the monotonicity of Riesz means with respect to the Robin parameter, the validity of these inequalities for a fixed β > 0 imply their validity for all larger values of β, and in particular the validity for the Dirichlet Laplacian. We believe that the inequalities of Theorem 1.3 extend to much larger classes of sets, but in what geometric generality and what range of γ they remain valid is far from clear. The most ambitious conjecture would be that these inequalities remain valid for all open Ω ⊂ Rd, d ≥ 2, of finite measure and all γ ≥ 0 as long as β is sufficiently large. Note that independently of β the stated bounds fail when d = 1 and 

γ = 0, in contrast to the case of the Dirichlet Laplace operator. The two-term asymptotics in Theorem 1.2 imply that β(γ, d ) ≥ βW (γ, d − 1). Further-more, we shall show below that it is the numbers β(γ, d ) from Theorem 1.3 that determine the transition point β∗ in Theorem 1.1 and in fact, β∗ = β(γ + 1 /2, d − 1). This might appear a bit surprising, however, this naturally emerges from the way that the uniform inequalities in Theorem 1.3 enter the proof of our main result. Specifically, they enter in order to exclude that sequences of maximizers collapse to a lower dimensional cuboid after appropriate rescaling. effective Robin spectral problem where the dimension is reduced and the order of Riesz mean increased. A similar ‘dimension drop’ is observed in [17] in the setting of Dirichlet and Neumann Laplace operators on convex sets. γ and d through the quantity γ + d/ 2. Thus, the claim that β∗ differs from the point where the second term in the asymptotics changes sign, i.e. β(γ + 1 /2, d − 1) > β W (γ, d − 1), is equivalent to β(γ + 1 /2, d − 1) > β W (γ + 1 /2, d − 2). In other words, the transition point

Outline of the strategy and structure of the paper. We end this introduction by outlin-ing the structure of the paper and our overall strategy. As mentioned earlier, the strategy runs parallel to that developed in [17]. An idea introduced in that paper is to divide the analysis into two regimes: (i) a semiclassical regime where uniform two-term spectral asymptotics are valid and (ii) a collapsing regime in which the shortest side length of a sequence of cuboids is not larger than the natural wavelength 1 /√λ as λ → ∞ . In this second regime, semiclassical approximations are not valid, but, as was observed in [17], one can still understand the limiting behavior as a partial semiclassical limit (see also [11]). The conclusion of Theorem 1.1 is then obtained by analyzing which of the two regimes is favorable for sequences of cuboids that asymptotically maximize the relevant Riesz means. A large part of our results rests on understanding the Robin eigenvalue problem on intervals and lifting the analysis to cuboids by using their product structure. As such, a key element of our analysis is to understand spectral properties of the one-dimensional Robin Laplacian. The required analysis of the one-dimensional problem is the topic of Section 2. The main result is a two-term asymptotic expansion of Riesz means of the Robin problem on an interval with a quantitatively controlled remainder estimate. In Section 3, the results from Section 2 are used to prove a version of Theorem 1.2 which is valid in the semiclassical regime explained above. The topic of Section 4 is to study the asymptotically collapsing regime. In Section 5 we prove Theorem 1.3. With Theorems 1.2 and 1.3 in hand we turn in Section 6 to the applications of these and analyze the asymptotic behavior of cuboids that maximize Mγ,d . We close the paper by showing that for some 

γ and d, the transition point β∗ in Theorem 1.1 indeed differs from the transition point 

βW (γ, d − 1) where the second term in the Weyl asymptotics changes sign. We also show numerical evidence that supports the conjecture that they never coincide.

7. Comparison of βW and β∗

We close the paper by analyzing the two values of β which play a central role in our analysis, namely, the critical Robin parameter β(γ, d ) from Theorem 5.1 and the point where the second term in the two-term Weyl formula changes sign, βW (γ, d ). Our main purpose is to demonstrate that, as we claimed in the introduction, there are d, γ, and β for which the heuristic argument for the asymptotic behavior of maximizers of Mγ,d (λ, β √λ)based on two-term asymptotics fails.
