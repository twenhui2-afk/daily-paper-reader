## Abstract
In 1953 LeVeque proved the existence of $U_m$-numbers by showing that for some specially defined Liouville number $λ$, the $m$th root $λ^{1/m}$ is in $U_m$. In this article we study the following question: let,$u$ be an algebraic function of degree $m$ and $λ$ a Liouville number; under which conditions is $u(λ)$ a $U_m$-number? We consider a more refined notion of $\mathcal{L}$-numbers, and show that, under very general assumptions, an algebraic function of degree $m$ takes $U_m$-values at all $\mathcal{L}$-numbers.
