WILLE LIU, WEI-HSUAN HSIN, AND CHENG-CHIANG TSAI 

Abstract. We prove that on a semisimple Lie algebra g over a finite field of large characteristic, if a complex-valued invariant function f and its Fourier transform ˆf are both supported in the nilpotent cone of g, then ˆf = γ−1f for an explicit quadratic Gauss sum γ. Consequently, we determine a fourth root of unity ap-pearing in various formulae of generalised Gel’fand–Graev characters, known as Lusztig constant, previously known in special cases due to works of Kawanaka, Digne–Lehrer–Michel, Waldspurger and Geck. As consequence, we show the va-lidity of a conjecture of Letellier on the compatibility of Fourier transform with Deligne–Lusztig induction.

The fourth root of unity ζ for which ˆf = ζf , called Lusztig constant (for a cuspidal block) in [Let05], has appeared in the study of characters of finite reduc-tive groups, notably in the calculation of generalised Gel’fand–Graev characters in Kawanaka [Kaw86] and Lusztig [Lus92]. Explicit formulae for ζ are previously known for G simple of types G2, F4 and E8 by Kawanaka [Kaw86] (see also Geck [Gec99]), An

by Digne–Lehrer–Michel [DLM92, DLM97], and classical groups SO(2 n + 1) , Sp(2 n)and SO(2 n) by Waldspurger [Wal01]. We calculate γψ(g) in § 2.4 for simple groups 

G and explain how Theorem 1 is compatible with these formulae. The proof of Theorem 1 proceeds by induction on dim G. The base cases are the trivial group G = 1 and types G2, F4 and E8, where the formula is known due to Kawanaka [Kaw86]. The inductive step relies mainly on Waldspurger’s works [Wal22, Wal25], which describe the endoscopic transfer of the space FC( g) for a p-adic Lie algebra g, as well as the commutation of Fourier transform with endoscopic transfer up to Weil indices, proved in [Wal95, Wal97] and conditioned on the validity of the fundamental lemma for Lie algebras, later verified by Ngˆ o [Ngˆ o10]. 5. On two conjectures of Letellier 

Resume to the setup of § 1. In [Let05], Letellier studies the relation between the Fourier transform and Deligne–Lusztig induction RGL : C(l)L → C(g)G on the space of invariant functions on Lie algebras. [Let05, p.2, Conjecture (*)] asserts that 

\(RGL f ) = εGεLRGL ˆf ∀ f ∈ C(l)L,

where εG = ( −1) rank Fq (G) and εL = ( −1) rank Fq (L). Letellier reduces [Let05, Theorem 6.2.16] this conjecture to some cases of another conjecture: Suppose ( O, C ) is a Fr-stable cuspidal pair on g. We have ζ(C ) the Lusztig constant in § 2.6. Let θ be a pinned automorphism of G, and θG the group over Fq with the twisted Frobenius. Suppose ( O, C ) is also θFr-stable. Then we can consider the Lusztig constant θζ(C )associated to C and θFr.

By Theorem 1, we are reduced to the observation εG ·γψ(g)−1 = εθ G ·γψ(θg)−1.This follows from Lemma 6 and that the determinant of θ on X∗(T) is equal to εG ·εθ G

by counting the θ-orbits in the Dynkin diagram of G. □

However, we would like to point out that Letellier’s original argument already suf-fices to prove [Let05, p.2, Conjecture (*)] when p > 3( h(G) − 1). It was observed in [Let05, §6.2.18] that a case-by-case analysis using results in [Kaw86], [DLM97], [Wal01] and [DLM03] resolves all but one case of [Let05, p.3, Conjecture (**)] neces-sary for proving [Let05, p.2, Conjecture (*)]. The last case is G = Spin(2 n) (possibly LUSZTIG CONSTANTS AND ENDOSCOPY 11 

non-split) and the cuspidal pair does not descend to SO(2 n). Such a situation does not in fact occur, as shown in the lemma below. 

Lemma 15. Let (O, C ) be a cuspidal pair on Spin(2 n) that does not arise from one on SO(2 n). Then there is exactly one Fq-structure on Spin(2 n) for which (O, C ) is stabilized by the Frobenius endomorphism. Proof. The statement follows immediately from the classification of Frobenius-stable cuspidal pairs, see [Wal25, §§ 2.6, 2.7]. We provide a proof here. By the classification of cuspidal pairs in [Lus84], we know that n̸ = 1 , 2, 4. In particular, Spin(2 n) admits exactly two Fq-structures up to isomorphism, the split form and the non-split form. Let χ be the central character associated with ( O, C ). Since ( O, C ) does not arise from SO(2 n), we have χ(ε) = −1, where ε denotes the generator of ker(Spin(2 n) → SO(2 n)) ∼= μ2.

Let z1, z 2 ∈ Z(Spin(2 n)) be the two remaining elements distinct from 1 and ε. Then 

z1 = εz 2, and hence χ(z1)̸ = χ(z2). On the other hand, among the split and non-split 

Fq-structures on Spin 2n, exactly one induces a non-trivial Frobenius action on the center Z(Spin(2 n)), swapping z1 and z2. Since ( O, C ) is stabilized by the Frobenius endomorphism if and only if its central character χ is, it follows that there is a unique 

Fq-structure for which ( O, C ) is stabilized by the Frobenius endomorphism. □
