Abstract 

This study examines the domination polynomials of friendship graphs and book graphs, focusing on unanswered questions related to these families. For the friend-ship graph Fn, with even n, we show that the polynomial D(Fn, x ) has exactly three real zeros: 0 and two simple zeros in the intervals ( −2, −1) and ( −1, 0). We further show that these two nonzero zeros have monotonic variation and converge to −1 − 1√2 and −1 + 1√2 , respectively. We obtain the quantitative approximation (|z| − 1) 2 log |z| ≤ n for any complex zeros of D(Fn, x ), resulting in the explicit bound |z| ≤ 1 + 

q n 

> log 2

. For book graphs Bn, we ascertain the comprehensive limit set of domination roots and establish results about the presence of real roots con-tingent on parity. We provide a partial answer to the integer-root an issue by establishing that friendship and book graphs have no nonzero integer domination roots, whereas for corona families, the only nonzero integer root is −2.

# 7 Conclusion and future work 

We have reexamined four unresolved inquiries on the domination polynomials of friend-ship and book graphs, yielding a combination of comprehensive and partial responses. For friendship graphs, the answer to Open Problem 2.6(i) is complete (Theorem 3.1): if n is even, then D(Fn, x ) has exactly three real roots, namely 0 and two simple roots in Root geometry of domination polynomials for friendship and book graphs 16 (−2, −1) and ( −1, 0). We also proved that these roots move monotonically and converge to the hyperbola intercepts −1± 1√2 (Corollary 3.2).
