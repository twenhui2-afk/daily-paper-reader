THE ANDERSEN-MASBAUM-UENO CONJECTURE FOR THE DERIVED SUBGROUP OF THE JOHNSON KERNEL 

RENAUD DETCHERRY 

Abstract. A conjecture of Andersen, Masbaum and Ueno states that for any compact oriented surface Σ g,n and any pseudo-Anosov 

f ∈ Mod(Σ g,n ), the matrix ρr (f ) has infinite order for any large r, 

where ρr is the SO(3)-WRT quantum representation of the map-ping class group Mod(Σ g,n ) at a primitive r-th root of unity. We prove this conjecture for prime r and any f ∈ [J2(Σ g,n ), J 2(Σ g,n )] ,

where J2(Σ g,n ) is the Johnson kernel. 

1. Introduction 

Let Σ g,n be a compact oriented surface of genus g and n boundary components. The (pure) mapping class group of Σ g,n is Mod(Σ g,n ) = Homeo +(Σ g,n )/Homeo 0(Σ g,n )where Homeo 0(Σ g,n ) stands for homeomorphisms that are isotopic to the identity, and we require homeomorphisms and isotopy to be the identity on the boundary. The Nielsen-Thurston classification separates mapping classes into 3 large types: finite order elements, reducible elements and pseudo-Anosov elements. Pseudo-Anosov elements stabilize two transverse singular foliations Fu, Fs on Σ g,n , contracting a transverse measure on Fs and dilating a transverse measure on Fu by a factor λ > 1.

They may also be characterized by their action on isotopy classes of simple closed curves having no finite orbit. Reducible maps stabilize (up to isotopy) a multicurve on Σ g,n . Up to taking a power, a reducible mapping class may be restricted to a partition of Σ g,n into subsurfaces; we then say that it has a pseudo-Anosov part if its restriction to one subsurface is pseudo-Anosov. Mapping class groups of surfaces enjoy a rich variety of linear repre-sentations, and a natural question is whether pseudo-Anosov maps may be described in terms of representations of the mapping class groups. To this end, a conjecture of Andersen, Masbaum and Ueno gives a

For any odd integer r ≥ 3, and any map 

c : |∂S | −→ Ir = {0, 2, . . . , r − 3},

the SO 3-WRT quantum representation ρr,c is a map: 

ρr,c : Mod(Σ g,n ) −→ PAut( RT r(S, c )) ,

where RT p(S, c ) is a Q(ζr)-vector space, where ζr is a primitive r-th root of unity, called the WRT TQFT-vector space. When S has bound-ary, we denote by ρr the direct sum of the representations ρr,c . The Andersen-Masbaum-Ueno conjecture (or AMU conjecture) is then the following: 

Conjecture 1. (AMU conjecture [AMU06] ) Let Σg,n be a compact ori-ented surface and f ∈ Mod(Σ g,n ). Then f has a pseudo-Anosov part if and only if ρr(f ) has infinite order for all large enough odd r ≥ 3.

It is not difficult to prove that when f has no pseudo-Anosov part, then ρr(f ) has finite order; this observation is what motivated the conjecture in [AMU06]. Moreover, it is not hard to show that the con-jecture would follow from the case of pseudo-Anosov mapping classes. The AMU conjecture has only been proved for a handful cases of surfaces S and pseudo-Anosov maps f. In [AMU06] and [San12], the conjecture is proved for pseudo-Anosov mapping classes of Mod(Σ 0,4)and Mod(Σ 1,1) respectively, by relating the quantum representation to the homology representation of the mapping class group. In [EJr16] and [San17], the conjecture is proved for certain mapping classes of Mod(Σ 0,n ) where n ≥ 5, by relating the quantum representation to homological representations on ramified covers of the surface Σ 0,n .

The first pseudo-Anosov examples of the AMU conjecture in genus at least two were obtained by March´ e and Santharoubane in [MS21]. Those examples belong to Mod(Σ g, 1) and are elements of the point-pushing subgroups coming from the Birman exact sequence. In [DK19], the author and Kalfgianni constructed many more pseudo-Anosov examples of the conjecture, belonging to Mod(Σ g,n ) where ei-ther n = 2 and g ≥ 3 or g ≥ n ≥ 2. The examples occured as monodromies of fibered links whose Turaev-Viro invariants grow expo-nentially. In a follow-up work [DK22], we extended this construction to provide pseudo-Anosov mapping classes in Mod(Σ g, 1) for g ≥ 9,

as monodromies of open book decompositions on 3-manifolds whose Turaev-Viro invariants grow exponentially. THE AMU CONJECTURE FOR THE DERIVED SUBGROUP OF J2(Σ g,n ) 3

We note that a common feature of all those examples is that they rely on asymptotic estimates of the ρr(f ) (and often, of their traces). Indeed, it is shown in [DK19] that the AMU conjecture would follow from the Chen-Yang volume conjecture [CY18]. In this article, we use an entirely different strategy, replacing analysis by number theory as our main tool. Let J2(Σ g,n ) be the Johnson ker-nel, that is, the subgroup of Mod(Σ g,n ) generated by Dehn twists along separating cruves. A celebrated theorem of Johnson [Joh85] charac-terizes this subgroup for n ≤ 1 as the subgroup that acts trivially on 

π1(Σ g,n )/Γ3(π1(Σ g,n )) , where Γk(G) = [ G, [G, [. . . , [G, G ]]]] (G appears k times). For k ≥ 1, and n ≤ 1, the k-th Johnson ker-nel was further defined to be the subgroup which acts trivially on 

π1(Σ g,n )/Γk+1 (π1(Σ g,n )) . We prove: 

Theorem 1.1. Let Σg,n be a compact oriented surface of genus g and 

n boundary components. Let f ∈ [J2(Σ g,n ), J 2(Σ g,n ))] be non-trivial. Then for any prime p large enough, ρp(f ) has infinite order. 

The above theorem in particular provides the first pseudo-Anosov examples of the AMU conjecture for closed surfaces. Moreover, it is the first time that the AMU conjecture is proved for a natural subgroup of the mapping class group. 

Remark 1.2. An immediate consequence of Theorem 1.1 is that any non-trivial element of [ J2(Σ g,n ), J 2(Σ g,n )] has a pseudo-Anosov part, since elements without a pseudo-Anosov part always have finite order images by the representations ρp.

While this fact is not surprising, the author was not able to locate a written proof of this in the litterature. It should however be possible to even prove that any non-trivial ele-ment of J3(Σ g) has a pseudo-Anosov part, using that Torelli groups are torsion-free (see [FM12, Theorem 6.12]) and showing that multitwists in the Torelli group have non-trivial image either by the first or second Johnson homomorphism. Theorem 1.1 also has the following corollary: 

Corollary 1.3. Let f ∈ Mod(Σ g,n ) be such that f has a pseudo-Anosov part and that f has finite order in Mod(Σ g,n )/[J2(Σ g,n ), J 2(Σ g,n )] . Then for any prime p large enough, ρp(f ) has infinite order. 

The corollary follows from Theorem 1.1 by applying it to f d where 

d is the order of f in Mod(Σ g,n )/[J2(Σ g,n ), J 2(Σ g,n )]; note that f d is non-trivial as f has a pseudo-Anosov part and hence infinite order. 4 RENAUD DETCHERRY 

Remark 1.4. One may construct pseudo-Anosov elements of Mod(Σ g,n )that are covered by Corollary 1.3 but not by Theorem 1.1 as fol-lows. Take f which is either a non-trivial finite order element of Mod(Σ g,n ), or an element of J2(Σ g,n ) representing non-trivial torsion in 

H1(J2(Σ g,n ), Z) (the latter exists by [NSS22], see also [FM26]). From [Wat20, Theorem 1.3], it is possible to find φ ∈ [J2(Σ g,n ), J 2(Σ g,n )] such that f φ k is a pseudo-Anosov element for large enough k. Moreover, this element represents a non-trivial torsion element in Mod(Σ g,n )/[J2(Σ g,n ), J 2(Σ g,n )] .

Our techniques also yield the following: 

Theorem 1.5. Let Σg,n be a compact oriented surface of genus g and 

n boundary components. Let f ∈ J2(Σ g,n ) be non-trivial. Then for any prime p ≥ 5 large enough, ρp(f ) has either order p or infinite order. 

We note that the above theorem is optimal, in the sense that separat-ing Dehn twists belong to J2(Σ g,n ) and are well-known to have images of order p by ρp.

The author wonders if one could prove a version of Theorem 1.1 for the third term J3(Σ g) of the Johnson filtration, since, according to Remark 1.2, any non-trivial element of J3(Σ g) has a pseudo-Anosov part. The proofs of Theorem 1.1 and 1.5 rely on a number-theoretic crite-rion (Propositions 2.1 and 2.7)for a matrix with entries in a cyclotomic ring to have infinite order, which we prove in Section 2. In addition, we make use of the integrality of the WRT representations ρp and the structure of their restriction to J2(Σ g,n ), which was studied in [GM07]. Finally, we make use of the asymptotic faithfulness of the WRT repre-sentations, due to Andersen [And06] and Freedman, Walker and Wang [FWW02].

3. The h-adic expansion and the AMU conjecture 

In this section, we let ρp be the Witten-Reshetikhin-Turaev SO 3-quantum representation of the mapping class group Mod(Σ g,n ) of a compact oriented surface Σ g,n of genus g and n boundary components. We refer to [BHMV95] and [GM07] for a definition. Following [GM07], the quantum representation ρp is a projective representation: 

ρp : Mod(Σ g,n ) −→ PAut( Sp(Σ g,n )) where Sp(Σ g,n ) is a free Z[ζp]-module of finite rank. The rank of 

Sp(Σ g,n ) may be explicitely computed using the Verlinde formula [BHMV95, Corollary 1.16]. We note that the module Sp(Σ g,n ) breaks into direct summands Sp(Σ g,n , c ), where c : |∂Σg,n | −→ { 0, 2 . . . , p − 3}, is a col-oration of the boundary components of Σ g,n . Moreover, ρp lifts to a linear representation of a central extension of Mod(Σ g,n ), with coef-ficients in a cyclotomic ring Z[ζp] or Z[ζ4p] depending on p mod 4; however as a projective representation it may always be considered to have Z[ζp]-coefficients.
