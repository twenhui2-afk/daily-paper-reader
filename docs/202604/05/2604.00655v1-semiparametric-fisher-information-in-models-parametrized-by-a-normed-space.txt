# Semiparametric Fisher Information in Models parametrized by a Normed Space 

Telmo P´ erez-Izquierdo ∗

April 2, 2026 

Abstract 

This paper studies semiparametric Fisher information in models parametrized by general normed spaces. The main contribution is to establish that positive semiparametric Fisher information is equiva-lent to the gradient of the parameter of interest lying in the range of the adjoint score operator. This result generalizes a key theorem Van Der Vaart (1991) and provides a unified framework linking differen-tiability and information, beyond Hilbert spaces. The paper develops a normed-space mean-square-differentiable models for two canonical problems: estimation of the average of a known transformation and estimation of a density at a point. In these applications, it shows that positive information holds if and only if the transformation has finite variance and if and only if the density has positive mass at the evaluation point, respectively. These findings offer a novel information-theoretic perspective on known minimax results and clarify the condi-tions under which root-n estimation is possible. 

Keywords: Semiparametric Fisher information; differentiable func-tionals; root-n estimation; score operator; efficiency bounds; mean es-timation; nonparametric density estimation.

The problem of asymptotic estimation and inference can be framed in a general setup where the goal is to recover a parameter β0 ≡ κ(P0), for a functional κ : P → R and a collection of distributions P, where P0 ∈ P 

is the true distribution of the data. The key question is how well one can estimate β0. In this setting, local asymptotic minimax and convolu-tion/representation theorems give lower bounds for the asymptotic variance

These results have been extended to nonparamet-ric and semiparametric models (Koshevnik and Levit, 1976; Begun et al., 1983; Van Der Vaart, 1991). A key insight in estimation of β0 is that of differentiability of the defin-ing functional κ (Pfanzagl, 1982; Van Der Vaart, 1991). Differentiability is a necessary condition for regular estimation of β0 at root-n rate (Van Der Vaart, 1991, Th. 2.1). Moreover, there is a stark link between root-n es-timability, differentiability, and positive semiparametric Fisher information. For models P = {Pλ : λ ∈ Λ} parametrized by a subset Λ of a Hilbert space, Van Der Vaart (1991) establishes that differentiability of κ(Pλ) ≡ ψ(λ), for a functional ψ : Λ → R, is equivalent to positive semiparametric Fisher in-formation for β0. Hence, positive information is a necessary condition for root-n estimation of β0 (see also Chamberlain, 1986). The above framework is quite general since, given a dominating measure one can parametrize P by the square-roots of the densities, which live in a Hilbert space. However, key results rely on mean-square differentiability of the model, which requires a continuous score operator, and on pathwise dif-ferentiability of ψ, which requires a continuous gradient. These conditions tend to hold for well-behaved parameters, but may fail when one attempts to show that a parameter is not root-n estimable. In such cases, certain smoothness conditions on the model readily lead to mean-square differentia-bility and a pathwise differentiable functional. Nevertheless, the restricted model ends up being parametrized by a general normed space, not a Hilbert space. The contribution of this paper is to generalize the link between positive information and differentiability to models parametrized by normed spaces. I extend Theorem 4.1 in Van Der Vaart (1991) to normed spaces, showing that positive semiparametric Fisher information is equivalent to the gradi-ent being in the range of the adjoint score operator. The usefulness of the extension is illustrated in two classical problems: estimation of the average of a known transformation and estimation of the density at a point. For both applications, I introduce a normed-space mean-square-differentiable model where the functionals of interest are pathwise differentiable with con-tinuous gradients. I find that semiparametric Fisher information is positive for estimation of the average of a known transformation if and only if the transformation has finite variance. For estimation of the density at a point, semiparametric Fisher information is positive if and only if there is positive mass at the point of interest. Up to my knowledge, these results are novel, even if they are aligned with the literature. There is a large recent literature on estimation of the mean for heavy-tailed distributions, which is closely related to estimation of the average of a known transformation with possibly infinite variance (for an overview, see Lugosi and Mendelson, 2019). For instance, Devroye et al. (2016, Th. 3.1) 2give a (non-asymptotic) minimax bound of n−s/ (1+ s) for estimation of the mean in the class of distributions with E 0[|g(X)|1+ s] ≤ C, with s ∈ (0 , 1]. On the other hand, slower than root-n rates for density estimation are well established. For instance, Ibragimov and Has’minskii (1981, Th. 5.1) give a minimax bound for estimation of n−s/ (2 s+1) , with s ≥ 1 being the smooth-ness of the class of densities (p. 235). My contribution is to link these results with semiparametric information theory, providing a standard way to obtain them. Zero information and non-differentiability results for the average of a known transformation and for the density at a point add up to the lit-erature on efficient semiparametric estimation. Chamberlain (1986) finds information bounds in semiparametric models with censoring. Ritov and Bickel (1990) discuss estimation of the squared integrated density, where information is positive but no root-n estimator exists. Khan and Tamer (2010) find zero information results for a semiparametric the binary choice model and treatment effects under exogenous selection. These authors use pathwise calculations to obtain their results. Closer to this paper, Van Der Vaart (1991) obtains results for mixing, censoring and truncation mod-els. Escanciano (2021) finds necessary and sufficient conditions for positive information for willigness to pay and average risk aversion. To obtain these results, they study the relative position of the gradient with respect to the range of the adjoint score operator. The rest of the paper is organized as follows. Section 2 introduces the setup and motivating examples. Section 3 gives an overview of the results. Section 4 contains the main result. Section 5 applies the main result to the examples. Section 6 concludes. All the proofs are in the Appendix.

# 4 Differentiability and positive information 

The proof strategy followed here discloses the link between positive informa-tion and the fact that ˙ψ ∈ R (A∗). The strategy relies on two ideas. First, I show that positive information and ˙ψ ∈ R (A∗) are linked to smoothness of the problem. That is, both concepts capture how easy it is to recover 

β0 = ψ(λ0) from the data pλ.To see this, it is useful to simplify the problem and consider one-to-one score operators. This establishes a bijection between the tangent space of the data R(A) and the tangent space of the parameter T . For each local deviation δ ∈ R (A) in the data, there is a unique local deviation α in the parameter. Therefore, one can locally recover λt = λ0 + tα + o(t) from the data, and the information in the model reduces to smoothness of the functionals involved. Of course, the one-to-one assumption on the score operator is very re-strictive. The second idea on which the proof is based is the following: if a local identifiability condition holds (see Van Der Vaart, 1991; Escanciano, 2021), then A is “sufficiently invertible”. By this I mean that A is invertible enough to locally recover ψ(λt). The precise meaning of this is made clear after the discussion of the one-to-one A case.

# 6 Conclusion 

This paper shows that, for models parametrized by arbitrary normed spaces, positive semiparametric information is equivalent to the gradient of the func-tional of interest being in the range of the adjoint score operator. This result extends the classical Hilbert-space result in Van Der Vaart (1991, Th. 4.1) to general normed spaces. The paper also proposes a normed-space modelling framework for estimation of the average of a known transformation and the density at a point.
